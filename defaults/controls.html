Table Controls

Basics

You can do most piece manipulations "manually" by pointing, clicking, and dragging, with left clicks popping pieces to the front and right clicks sending them to the back. Shift-clicking will let you select multiple pieces.

You can also change your view by clicking and dragging the table, using the arrow keys. Shift + arrow keys will allow you to rotate and zoom the table. The mouse wheel also allows for zooming, or (with shift) rotating.

Below are some more advanced controls. I highly recommend slowly learning these because they really save a lot of effort in the long run. A very nice approach is to center one hand on the WASD keys while the other uses the mouse. Setting and recalling views with the number keys is also very useful!

Table View

Zoom: Wheel, +/-, Shift + W/S, Shift + Up/Down
Rotate: Shift + Mouse Wheel, Q/E, Shift + A/D, Shift + Left/Right
Pan: Drag, W/A/S/D, Arrow Keys
Recenter: Escape, 0
View Shortcuts: 1-9
Set Current View Shortcut: Shift + 1-9
Focus / Quickzoom: F (Shift + F to Focus on Selected Piece

Selecting Pieces

Select and Bring to Top: Click
Select in Place: Right Click
Select Multiple: Right Click or Shift + Click on Table and Drag
Add to / Toggle Selection: Shift + Click or Drag Selection Window

When pieces are selected

Rotate: Shift + Mouse Wheel, Shift + A/D, Shift + Left/Right
Flip: Double Click, Space (works on piece under the mouse with no selection)
Move: Drag
Collect / Stack: C
Xpand / Xpose: X (Shift + X to Sort)
Zhuffle: Z (works with mouse hovering and no selection)
